How to Write Website Content That AI Tools Actually Cite

Chris · June 2026 · 9 min read

AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews don't cite every website equally. Some pages get referenced constantly — their answers quoted, their links surfaced, their business recommended. Others sit invisible, even if they rank well in traditional Google. The difference comes down to how the content is written, not just what it covers. This is what GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation) actually means in practice.

Why AI tools skip most websites

AI tools are in the extraction business. When someone asks a question, the AI's job is to pull a precise, direct answer out of the vast web and present it confidently. It needs content that answers the question cleanly — no preamble, no hedging, no "it depends" without a clear follow-up explanation.

Think of AI as a very fast journalist on deadline. It needs a quote — a direct, quotable answer it can lift and use. Content that meanders before getting to the point doesn't get cited. The first paragraph that actually answers the question does.

Studies comparing cited vs. uncited content consistently find that cited pages do a handful of things that uncited pages don't. None of them require technical expertise. They're writing choices.

The seven characteristics of AI-citable content

01

Answer-first structure

The direct answer appears in the first sentence or paragraph — not after context-setting. AI tools extract the first clear answer they find.

02

Question-format headings

Sections titled as real questions ("How long does a boiler service take?") signal to AI exactly what question this section answers.

03

Specific numbers and facts

AI tools prefer concrete data over vague claims. "Takes 45 minutes" beats "takes a short time." Statistics become citation anchors.

04

FAQPage schema markup

Wrapping Q&A content in FAQPage JSON-LD tells AI tools exactly which section is a question and which is the answer — no guesswork.

05

Direct, confident language

Hedge words ("possibly," "might," "could be") lower AI confidence in your content. State what's true directly.

06

Local specificity

Mentioning your town, area, and service specifics makes your content the best answer for local queries — the ones most likely to convert.

Answer-first: the single biggest shift to make

Most business websites bury the answer. They open with history, credentials, or background. They build to the answer. AI tools don't wait. Here's what the difference looks like:

❌ Not citable — answer buried

"At Smith Plumbing, we've been serving Birmingham homeowners for over 15 years. We pride ourselves on our fast response times and qualified engineers. If you're wondering how quickly we can get to you for an emergency callout, we try to be there as soon as possible, usually within a few hours depending on our schedule."

✓ Citable — answer first

"Smith Plumbing provides emergency plumber callouts across Birmingham within 60 minutes, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Our engineers are Gas Safe registered and carry common parts on the van to fix most problems on the first visit."

The second version leads with a specific, extractable answer — response time, coverage area, operating hours, and a differentiator. An AI tool can cite that. It can quote "within 60 minutes, 24/7" directly. The first version has nothing to quote.

How to write question-format headings

Your headings are signposts for AI tools as much as for human readers. Turn your service description headings into questions your customers actually ask:

  • "How much does a boiler service cost in Birmingham?" instead of "Our Boiler Service Prices"
  • "How long does a bathroom installation take?" instead of "Bathroom Installation Timeline"
  • "Do you offer emergency callouts on weekends?" instead of "Emergency Availability"

Each question-format heading tells an AI tool: "this section answers this specific question." When someone asks that question, your page becomes a strong citation candidate.

The FAQ section: your most powerful citation asset

A dedicated FAQ section — with real questions written the way customers ask them, and direct answers — is the single most effective thing you can add to a service page for AI citation. Pair it with FAQPage schema markup and you've done everything right.

The questions should come from reality: the questions you get on the phone, by WhatsApp, in Google reviews, in the Q&A section of your GBP. These are questions real people are actually asking — which means AI tools are also being asked them.

Quick win

Add 5 FAQ questions to your homepage or main service page this week. Write the questions as your customers ask them. Lead each answer with the direct answer. Add FAQPage JSON-LD schema. That's the fastest route to AI citations for a local business website.

Specific numbers: the citation anchor

AI tools love specific, quotable data. Vague claims ("we're quick," "we're affordable," "most customers are happy") provide nothing to cite. Specific claims do:

  • "We've completed over 800 boiler installations across Birmingham since 2019"
  • "98% of our emergency callouts arrive within 90 minutes"
  • "Our average review score is 4.9 stars from 143 Google reviews"
  • "We carry over 200 standard parts on every van to fix most boiler faults on the first visit"

These aren't just good marketing — they're citation anchors. An AI tool presenting you as a recommendation can quote specific claims. Vague claims don't quote.

Local specificity: be the best answer for local queries

Generic service pages compete with every national and regional site in the country. Local-specific pages compete with a much smaller pool — and win far more often when the AI is matching someone's local query to the best available source.

Mention your town, your neighbourhood coverage, local landmarks, and specific local context where relevant. "We cover the whole of Birmingham, including Moseley, Kings Heath, Harborne, and Edgbaston" beats "we cover the West Midlands area." The first creates multiple citation opportunities for someone searching in a specific neighbourhood.

What to do with your existing pages

You don't need to rewrite everything. Pick one service page — ideally your most important one — and do this:

  1. Rewrite the first paragraph to lead with a direct, specific answer to "what do you do and where?"
  2. Change at least 3 headings to question format
  3. Add a 5-question FAQ section at the bottom with direct answers
  4. Add FAQPage JSON-LD schema wrapping those questions
  5. Include at least 3 specific numbers or facts (years in business, review count, response time, job count)

Then test it. Ask ChatGPT and Perplexity the questions your FAQ answers. Come back in 4-6 weeks and test again. The improvement is usually visible within 6-8 weeks as AI tools re-crawl and re-index your content.

Frequently asked questions

What type of content do AI tools cite most?

AI tools most frequently cite content that gives direct, specific answers to questions — particularly FAQ sections with clear Q&A formatting, how-to guides with numbered steps, and pages with FAQPage or HowTo schema markup. Content that buries the answer in background context is far less likely to be cited.

Does content length affect whether AI tools cite my website?

Length alone doesn't determine AI citation. What matters is whether your content answers a specific question directly and completely. A 300-word FAQ answer that directly addresses a question is more likely to be cited than a 2,000-word article that mentions the answer in passing.

How do I know if AI tools are citing my website?

Ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews direct questions that your website content answers. Perplexity shows citations directly. For ChatGPT, check whether your business or website URL appears in the response.

What is GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation)?

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimisation — writing and structuring content so that AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews can extract and cite it in their responses. Unlike traditional SEO which focuses on keyword rankings, GEO focuses on being the source AI tools quote.
