How to Do a Local AI Search Audit in 30 Minutes

Chris · June 2026 · 8 min read

A local AI search audit tells you exactly how visible your business is to ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews — and more importantly, what's holding you back. The whole thing takes 30 minutes and doesn't require any technical tools. You just need a browser, five things to check, and a notepad to write down what needs fixing.

Why a structured audit beats guessing

Most businesses try to improve AI visibility by doing one thing — updating their Google Business Profile, or getting more reviews — and then wondering why nothing changed. AI tools make recommendations based on a combination of signals. Fixing one weak signal while ignoring four others rarely moves the needle.

Think of it like a job interview. A perfect CV doesn't compensate for a terrible interview. A great interview doesn't overcome a CV with no references. AI tools are running the same checklist — and you need to pass all five sections, not just one.

A local AI search audit isn't about gaming the system. It's about making sure AI tools have complete, accurate, consistent information about your business — so when someone asks for a recommendation, you're a confident answer, not an uncertain maybe.

Step 1: Test yourself right now (5 minutes)

Before auditing anything, find out where you actually stand. Open ChatGPT and Perplexity and run these exact searches — replace the brackets with your actual details:

  • "Who are the best [your trade] in [your town]?"
  • "Can you recommend a [your service type] near [your area]?"
  • "What's a good [your business category] in [your town] — something local people would recommend?"

Write down what comes back. Do you appear? Do competitors appear instead? Is the information about you (if you appear) accurate? This baseline tells you whether you have a visibility problem, an accuracy problem, or no problem at all.

Set the baseline

Screenshot or copy the AI responses. You'll want to compare against them in 6-8 weeks after making improvements — that's the only reliable way to measure whether the changes worked.

Step 2: Audit your Google Business Profile (10 minutes)

Your GBP is the primary source most AI tools use for local business data. Open business.google.com and check each of these:

A

Business name, address, and phone

Exact match — no abbreviations, no inconsistencies between what's here and what's on your website and other directories. "St." vs "Street" matters. "Ltd" vs "Limited" matters.

B

Business categories

Have you selected a primary category and relevant secondary categories? Many businesses only pick one. A plumber should have "Plumber" as primary plus "Emergency Plumber," "Gas Engineer," and "Heating Contractor" as additional categories where relevant.

C

Services list

Is your Services section fully completed? This is where you list specific services with descriptions. Each one adds indexed keywords to your profile. Blank services section = missed opportunity.

D

Attributes

Attributes are the small tick-box options (free Wi-Fi, wheelchair accessible, women-led, etc.). They feed AI tools with specific information about your business that goes beyond category and location.

E

Photos

At minimum: your logo, exterior photo, and 3-4 photos of your work or premises. Profiles with 10+ photos receive significantly more visibility in local search. AI tools use photo presence as an engagement and activity signal.

F

Recent posts and Q&A

When did you last post? If it was more than 2 weeks ago, your activity signal is weakening. Is your Q&A section populated? If empty, seed it with 5 questions your customers regularly ask.

Step 3: Audit your citation consistency (5 minutes)

Citations are the digital equivalent of a formal reference letter — every directory that lists your business with consistent information adds credibility to the AI's confidence in recommending you. Open a new tab and search:

  • Your business name on Yelp UK
  • Your business name on Yell.com
  • Your business name on Facebook
  • Your business name on Apple Maps (search in Safari on an iPhone, or maps.apple.com)

For each one, check: does the name, address, and phone number match your GBP exactly? Is the category set correctly? Is there a website link? Note any discrepancies — even minor ones need fixing.

Step 4: Check your review signals (5 minutes)

Open your Google Business Profile public listing (search your business name in Google and look at the panel). Check:

  • Total review count — how does this compare to your top 3 local competitors in the same trade?
  • Most recent review — was it in the last 4 weeks? If not, your velocity signal is declining.
  • Response rate — have you replied to every review? Scroll through. Any unanswered ones need a response today.
  • Review content quality — do your recent reviews describe specific jobs, locations, and outcomes? Or are they mostly star ratings with no text? Thin reviews are worth less to AI than detailed ones.

Step 5: Audit your website for AI readability (5 minutes)

This is the area most businesses haven't touched. Right-click your website homepage, choose "View Page Source," and search for LocalBusiness. If you find it, you have schema markup — check that the details are current and accurate. If you don't find it, you're missing the signal that AI tools most rely on to understand who you are.

Also check:

  • Does your homepage have a clear, direct answer to "what does this business do and where?" in the first paragraph?
  • Does your site have a FAQ section? Are the questions written the way customers would actually ask them?
  • Is your phone number and address visible on the page (not just in the footer)?

Scoring your audit

Critical
GBP incomplete (missing categories, services, or photos) — this is limiting everything else. Fix this first.
Critical
NAP inconsistency across directories — AI confidence in recommending you drops when your details don't match.
Important
No LocalBusiness schema on website — you're missing the clearest signal AI tools use to identify you.
Important
Review velocity low (fewer than 4 reviews in last 90 days) — freshness signal is weakening.
Opportunity
No FAQ section on website — adding FAQPage schema is the fastest route to improved AI citations.
Opportunity
Apple Maps listing incomplete or unclaimed — 29% of UK smartphone users default to Apple Maps.

What to fix first

Prioritise in this order:

  1. Fix any GBP gaps — categories, services, photos, attributes. This is your highest-leverage 30 minutes.
  2. Fix citation inconsistencies — update every directory where your NAP doesn't match exactly.
  3. Add LocalBusiness schema to your homepage if it's missing.
  4. Get your review velocity moving — implement the 3-step ask system (verbal → WhatsApp → QR code).
  5. Add FAQ content with schema to your website — 3-5 questions per key service page.

Come back in 8 weeks and run Step 1 again. Compare the AI responses to your baseline. That's your proof of progress.

Frequently asked questions

What is a local AI search audit?

A local AI search audit is a structured check of how visible your business is to AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. It covers your Google Business Profile completeness, citation consistency, review quality, website schema markup, and content structure — the five factors that determine whether AI tools recommend your business.

How do I check if my business appears in ChatGPT?

Open ChatGPT and ask: "Who are the best [your trade] in [your town]?" and "Can you recommend a [your service] near [your location]?" If your business doesn't appear, work through the five audit areas — GBP completeness, citation consistency, review quality, schema markup, and FAQ content.

How long does it take to improve AI search visibility?

Improvements to AI search visibility typically take 4-8 weeks to reflect in AI recommendations. Google AI Overviews can update faster — within 1-2 weeks of Google re-crawling your site. The audit identifies quick wins (GBP gaps, missing schema) that can be fixed in a day and produce results within 4-6 weeks.

What is the most important factor for local AI search visibility?

A complete and verified Google Business Profile is the single most important factor for local AI search visibility. It's the primary source most AI tools use for local business data. After that, consistent citations across directories, quality review content, and LocalBusiness schema on your website all significantly improve your chances of being cited.
