What Is GEO? Generative Engine Optimisation Explained for Beginners

Chris · June 2026 · 6 min read

GEO — Generative Engine Optimisation — is the practice of making your content, products, or business visible to AI search engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Microsoft Copilot. Where traditional SEO was about ranking in a list of blue links, GEO is about being cited, quoted, or recommended inside an AI-generated answer. The same way SEO became a core skill for anyone with a website in the 2000s, GEO is becoming essential for anyone who wants to be found online in 2026.

GEO vs SEO — what's actually different?

Think of traditional SEO like getting your shop onto a busy high street. The goal was to appear in the right place on the right page so people walking past would see you. You competed for position — rank 1 got the foot traffic, rank 10 got almost none.

GEO is more like becoming the business that local experts spontaneously recommend. When someone asks a well-connected friend "who should I use for X?", that friend doesn't consult a list — they think of the businesses they've heard recommended consistently, by trusted sources, in clear terms. AI search works the same way. It synthesises everything it knows about your business and decides whether to recommend you based on the weight of evidence, not just your ranking.

"GEO is the discipline of engineering your content to be discovered, understood, and cited by AI search systems — not just found by human readers."

The practical differences are significant. With SEO, you optimised one page for one keyword. With GEO, you're building a consistent identity across the entire web — your website, third-party mentions, reviews, social profiles, directories, and press coverage all contribute to what AI systems believe about you.

Why GEO matters right now in 2026

AI search engines now handle an estimated 12-18% of English-language informational queries. That percentage is growing fast. More importantly, the Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO) software category grew over 2,000% as businesses started noticing their pipeline was drying up despite stable Google rankings.

That last point is the key one. Businesses are maintaining their traditional rankings while losing customers — because those customers are asking AI assistants instead of clicking Google results. The two systems have overlapping but distinct visibility. You can rank number one on Google for a keyword and still be completely invisible when someone asks ChatGPT the same question.

The four pillars of GEO

1. Entity clarity — being unambiguously you

AI systems build a model of your business from everything they can find. The clearer and more consistent that information is, the more confident the AI becomes in recommending you. This means your business name, location, services, and key details should be identical across your website, Google Business Profile, social profiles, and every directory listing.

Inconsistency is the enemy. "John's Plumbing Ltd" and "John's Plumbing" and "J&M Plumbing" all look like different entities to an AI — even if they're all you.

2. Content structure — writing so AI can extract and cite you

AI systems don't read your content the way a human does — they scan for extractable signals. A clear answer in the first paragraph. Specific facts and numbers. Declarative sentences ("X is Y because Z"). FAQ sections with direct, answerable questions. Headings that describe what's in the section rather than just labelling it.

Writing well for GEO and writing well for humans aren't in conflict — in fact, GEO forces you to be more direct and specific, which most content benefits from anyway.

3. Cross-platform presence — being corroborated independently

AI platforms gain confidence in a business when the same positive signal appears from multiple independent sources. One great website isn't enough. You need mentions in credible, independent places: industry publications, review platforms, local press, relevant Reddit threads, professional directories, YouTube content if applicable.

This is the slowest part of GEO to build, and the hardest to fake — which is also why it's the most durable advantage.

4. Technical signals — structured data and crawlability

Schema markup is the vocabulary that tells AI systems what your content actually is — product, article, business, review, FAQ. Without it, an AI might be able to read your page but can't confidently categorise what it's reading. For e-commerce businesses in particular, schema markup is the difference between being in the structured data feeds AI platforms pull from and being invisible to them entirely.

The GEO checklist in brief

Consistent entity data everywhere · Direct answers in the first paragraph · Specific facts AI can quote · FAQ sections · Mentions on credible independent sites · Schema markup on key pages. That's GEO in a checklist.

How to start with GEO today

The easiest place to start is your own content. Pick your five most important web pages — homepage, key service or product pages — and read the first paragraph of each. Does it answer the most obvious question a visitor would have? Does it include specific facts and your location? Can you imagine an AI quoting that first paragraph verbatim? If not, rewrite it so it can.

Then search for yourself on Perplexity and ChatGPT. Ask the questions your customers would ask. See what comes up. If it's not you, look at what is. That's your benchmark.

Frequently asked questions

What does GEO stand for?

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimisation. It refers to the practice of optimising your online presence to be cited, recommended, or quoted by AI-powered search engines and assistants such as ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Microsoft Copilot.

Is GEO replacing SEO?

Not replacing — extending. Traditional SEO (ranking in Google's blue links) still matters and still drives significant traffic. GEO is an additional layer that addresses the growing portion of searches now handled by AI systems. Most good GEO practice also reinforces traditional SEO, so they're not in conflict.

Do I need a big website to benefit from GEO?

No. Small, clear, well-structured websites with accurate business information often outperform sprawling corporate sites in AI citations — because clarity beats volume. A local business with a clean website, a complete Google Business Profile, and genuine reviews on multiple platforms can outperform much larger competitors in AI recommendations.

How long does GEO take to show results?

Content structure improvements (rewriting your homepage, adding FAQ sections) can show results in Perplexity within days. Building cross-platform presence takes weeks to months. ChatGPT's training data updates are periodic, so influencing training-based responses takes longer. Think of GEO as a 90-day foundation-building project with ongoing maintenance.

Is GEO only relevant for certain types of businesses?

No — AI search usage is growing across every category. Local businesses, e-commerce stores, freelancers, B2B service providers, content creators — every type of business that needs to be found online is affected by the shift to AI search. The specific tactics vary by business type, but the need applies universally.
